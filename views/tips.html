🎓 Student Survival Guide

Everything you need to know to settle into life at the University of Westminster and London. From transport to banking to student discounts.

🚀 Getting Started in London
Essential first steps for every new Westminster student
🚇

Get Your Student Oyster Card

Apply for an 18+ Student Oyster photocard to get 30% off all TfL travel including the Tube, bus, Overground and Elizabeth line. Apply online through TfL before you arrive if possible.

Apply on TfL ↗
🏦

Open a UK Bank Account

You'll need a UK bank account to receive your student loan and pay rent. Monzo and Starling are the easiest to open with just your passport — no proof of address needed. Traditional banks like Barclays and HSBC offer student accounts with free railcards.

Try Monzo ↗
🏥

Register with a GP

Register with a local GP (doctor) as soon as you arrive. You're entitled to free NHS healthcare as a student. Westminster has several GP practices near campus — try the Marylebone Health Centre on Marylebone Road or search NHS.uk for practices accepting new patients.

Find a GP ↗
📱

Get a UK SIM Card

Pick up a UK SIM card at any phone shop or supermarket. SMARTY, Giffgaff and Three offer great value plans from £10/month with plenty of data. Most are rolling monthly contracts so no long-term commitment needed.

Try SMARTY ↗
💰 Student Discounts & Saving Money
Make your student loan go further with these discounts
🪪

TOTUM / NUS Card

Get a TOTUM card (formerly NUS Extra) for discounts at hundreds of retailers including ASOS, Co-op, and many restaurants. Costs around £14.99/year and pays for itself quickly.

Get TOTUM ↗
🍕

Cheap Eats Near Campus

Use apps like Too Good To Go for heavily discounted food near closing time. Lidl and Aldi on Edgware Road are the cheapest supermarkets near campus. Borough Market (20 min by tube) is great for cheap lunch on Saturdays.

Too Good To Go ↗
🎬

Entertainment Discounts

Use your Westminster student email to get Spotify Student (£5.99/month includes Hulu), Apple Music Student, and Amazon Prime Student (6 months free then 50% off). Cinema: Odeon and Vue both offer student discounts on weekdays.

Prime Student ↗
🚂

16-25 Railcard

If you're under 26, get a 16-25 Railcard for £30/year. It gives you 1/3 off all National Rail fares across the UK — perfect for visiting home or travelling around England. Pays for itself after just one or two trips.

Get Railcard ↗
📚 Study & Campus Life
Make the most of what Westminster has to offer
📖

Best Study Spots

The Marylebone Library (5th floor) is the best study spot on campus — arrive early for the best seats. Outside campus, try the British Library (free entry, stunning reading rooms) or any Wetherspoons with a laptop.

British Library ↗
💻

Microsoft 365 for Free

All Westminster students get Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams) completely free. Log in at office.com with your @westminster.ac.uk email. You can install it on up to 5 devices.

Get Office 365 ↗
🧠

Student Wellbeing Support

Westminster offers free counselling and mental health support to all students. The Student Wellbeing team can be contacted through the student portal. Don't wait until you're struggling — reach out early.

Wellbeing Support ↗
🏋️

Student Gym Membership

Westminster students get discounted gym membership at the university sports facilities. Alternatively, PureGym and The Gym Group offer flexible no-contract memberships from £18/month across London.

PureGym Student ↗
✅ First Week Checklist
Make sure you tick these off in your first week
💡

Pro tip

Print or screenshot this checklist and work through it systematically. Students who complete these steps in the first week settle in significantly faster and avoid common pitfalls later on.

  • ✓Collect your student ID card from the Student Centre
  • ✓Set up your @westminster.ac.uk email and log into Blackboard (the university's learning platform)
  • ✓Apply for your Student Oyster card online
  • ✓Open a UK bank account (Monzo or Starling for quickest setup)
  • ✓Register with a local GP near your accommodation
  • ✓Get a UK SIM card
  • ✓Attend your course induction and meet your Personal Tutor
  • ✓Visit the Student Union and find out what societies are on offer
  • ✓Download the TfL app and Citymapper for getting around London
  • ✓Activate Microsoft 365 with your university email
  • ✓Save the university emergency number: 020 7911 5000
